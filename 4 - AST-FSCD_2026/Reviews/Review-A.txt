Paper summary
The authors extend the functional machine calculus, a calculus derived from on a probabilistic λ-calculus but with added imperative constructs, with a type system that guarantees almost sure termination of all well-typed terms. In contrast to prior works, an iteration primitive is added. To guarantee almost-sure termination, the type system requires that each step of an iteration terminates with at least some fixed probability. The authors then study a version of this calculus restricted to “ground terms”, i.e., terms limited to choice, iteration, and probabilistic branching. They find that these terms correspond to finite Markov Chains and conclude that type checking for such terms is thus decidable.  Moreover, while this is not the case for the unrestricted version of the calculus, AST (termination with probability one) and PAST (finite expected runtime) coincide on ground terms.

Comments for authors
Overall the paper is well-written, the calculus and the presented concepts are illustrated with examples which makes it easy to follow. The concept of iteration fits well with the functional machine calculus and the proposed type system. This extension to existing work, consisting of iteration and the choice labels, while natural, is rather small.

I would like to see a discussion on the limits of type checking and the discussion on the calculus' limits to be accompanied by a small example that cannot be typed given the current rules (due to fixed exit probability).  Then, one could detail how the proposed extensions might solve this limitation.

## Pros
- natural extension of previous work on types for almost-sure termination with the FMC
- the paper is well-written and clear

## Cons

- the main contribution and idea of this paper seems to be a rather small extension of previous works

## Detailed Comments

Initially, I therefore had difficulty understanding the outlined type system until I found the corresponding remark in [15].
